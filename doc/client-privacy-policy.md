This policy is intended to comply with the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and the Italian AGCOM guidelines for age verification.

## 1. Data Controller and Processor

- **Data Controller**: [Your Website Name], [contact email], [address]
- **Data Processor**: Age Gate as a Service (operated by [Processor Name], [processor contact email])

## 2. What Data We Collect and Process

When you attempt to access age‑restricted content on our website, the Age Gate service processes the following information:

- **Your IP address** (anonymised immediately – only a non‑reversible hash is stored)
- **The result of the age verification** (whether you are over the required age threshold)
- **The threshold used** (e.g., 18, 21, 25)
- **Timestamp of the verification**

We do **not** collect your name, email address, identification document, or any other personal identifier.

## 3. Why We Process Your Data (Legal Basis)

The processing is necessary for the following legitimate interests (Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR):
- To comply with legal obligations to prevent underage access to age‑restricted content (e.g., AGCOM requirements).
- To ensure the security and integrity of our service (e.g., rate limiting, abuse prevention).
- To maintain audit records for compliance and troubleshooting.

We have balanced these interests against your rights and have determined that the processing is proportionate and does not override your privacy rights, because the data is anonymised and retention is limited to 30 days.

## 4. How Long We Keep Your Data

Verification records are automatically deleted after **30 days**. This period is chosen to allow us to detect patterns of abuse while minimising data retention.

## 5. Sharing Your Data

We do not sell or share your personal data with third parties, except:
- The Age Gate service (as a data processor) which processes the data on our behalf.
- If required by law or a court order.

The Age Gate service does not share the data further. All processing is carried out within the European Economic Area (EEA) unless otherwise stated.

## 6. Your Rights

Under GDPR, you have the following rights:

- **Right to access**: You can request a copy of the data we hold about you.
- **Right to rectification**: You can ask us to correct inaccurate data.
- **Right to erasure**: You can ask us to delete your data (but note that we only store anonymised data, so this right may be automatically satisfied).
- **Right to restrict processing**: You can ask us to stop processing your data.
- **Right to object**: You can object to processing based on legitimate interests.
- **Right to data portability**: You can request a machine‑readable copy of your data.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us at [privacy@yourdomain.com]. We will respond within one month.

## 7. How to Lodge a Complaint

If you believe that your data protection rights have been violated, you have the right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority. In Italy, this is the Garante per la protezione dei dati personali (www.garanteprivacy.it).

## 9. Data Security
